Pixel look mechanics

- Keep Pixel as a compact whole-body calico cat with the same rounded torso, short legs, triangular ears, large green eyes, short tail, and bold orange-black patches.
- The torso, paw placement, and overall body scale should stay nearly fixed across look directions; direction changes should read mostly through head, face, ear angle, tail curl bias, and slight shoulder rotation.
- The eyes rotate together as one pair and stay oversized and readable at pet scale. Up looks should place the pupils higher; down looks should place them lower; side looks should bias both pupils clearly toward the facing side without splitting them in different directions.
- Head turns should carry the ears, muzzle, whiskers, and patch placement with them so the face feels like one volume rather than a pasted-on front view.
- `000` means looking up, not neutral front. `090` means screen-right profile bias. `180` means looking down toward the viewer’s lower edge. `270` means screen-left profile bias.
- Up and down should keep the body readable and centered, with only modest foreshortening. Left and right can show a stronger cheek and muzzle bias, but do not let Pixel flatten into a completely different side-view cat.
- Preserve the same clean dark outline, chunky pixel clusters, limited warm palette, and crisp hard edges used in the standard rows.
- Do not include the laptop or any new prop in the look-direction rows; these rows are pure look semantics, not state actions.
- Tail, ear tilt, and patch silhouettes may shift slightly to support the turn, but registration, baseline, and cell padding must remain stable across neighboring directions.
